Putting the S back in IoT

The Introduction

At Home

Ottopress is a new take on the interconnected smart-home. Motivated by the lack of security and large barriers to entry that currently exist in home IoT solutions, Ottopress is designed with security, privacy and resilience in mind, ensuring that your home stays yours.


Through a cutting-edge approach to contextual awareness and identification, Ottopress can make situational inferences in commands, making your interaction with your home easier than flipping a light switch.

For Creators

Ottopress features an elegant and easy-to-use API (Application Program Interface) to allow creators, developers, and manufacturers to easily integrate their IoT products with the Ottopress framework.


The Journey

Summer 2016

The Inception

It was at the start of a day-long road trip with my two best friends that I began to form the idea that would become Ottopress. Just after imagining the basics of what I would want a smarthome to achieve, I shared the concept with Leif and Aidan. Everyone agreed there was something there, we just weren't sure what. What, at its start, was shaping to be a car ride of smartphones and silence, shifted to an incredible discussion on what needed to be solved in the IoT industry, and how we could do it.


By the time we had arrived at our hotel, I knew that this was what I wanted to pursue for my quest project, and the question became how.


School 2016-2017

Work in Progress

At this point, I knew what I was doing. The idea for modularization and building a network was done. This marked my launch into building the rest of the framework. Through this process, new ideas came and went. The project changed shape quite a few times as I learned more about the various fields: audio processing, machine learning, indoor localization, networking, security, android development patterns, and believe it or not, more.


Quest 2017

Send Help

Come quest night, I had to do quite a lot of thinking as to the presentation of my work. I had to pick whether or not to use a poster board, how I would display the Android application, how I would show people the interconnectivity of the devices, and even tuning the voice recognition to the noise in my expo room. While I will undoubtedly continue the project until I think it's finished, this marked a significant milestone as the first public presentation of my work.
